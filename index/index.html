Sri Lanka Qualifications Framework (SLQF)

Sri Lanka Qualifications Framework (SLQF)

National framework that enhances education quality, supports academic mobility and contributes to both individual growth and national socio-economic development.

Structure of the SLQF

For further details visit UGC Website

  • 10 Levels: Increasing complexity and learning outcomes.
    • Levels 1-2: Senior secondary education qualifications.
    • Levels 3-6: Undergraduate qualifications (Certificates, Diplomas, Bachelor's).
    • Levels 7-10: Postgraduate qualifications (Postgraduate Certificates, Diplomas, Master's, MPhil, PhD).
  • Integrated with the National Vocational Qualifications Framework (NVQF).

SLQF L-1-4: Secondary and Diploma

  • Level 1: Certificate
    • What it means: Basic knowledge in a discipline.
    • Example: Certificate in Catering (minimum 30 credits).
  • Level 2: Advanced Certificate
    • What it means: Generalized vocational knowledge.
    • Example: Advanced Certificate in Hospitality Management.
  • Level 3: Diploma
    • What it means: Focused vocational skills and in-depth knowledge in a specific field.
    • Example: Diploma in Hospitality Management.
  • Level 4: Higher Diploma
    • What it means: Intensive and focused education meeting labor market requirements.
    • Example: Higher Diploma in Accountancy (total 60 credits, with at least 30 at Level 4).

SLQF L-5-7: Undergraduate and Entry-Level Postgraduate

  • Level 5: Bachelor's Degree
    • What it means: General undergraduate education; foundational academic knowledge.
    • Examples: Bachelor of Arts (General) in Peace and Conflict Resolution, Bachelor of Science (General) in Environmental Management (minimum 90 credits).
  • Level 6: Honours Bachelor's Degree
    • What it means: Specialized and research-oriented undergraduate education.
    • Examples: Bachelor of Science Honours in Engineering, Bachelor of Arts Honours in Sociology (minimum 120 credits).
  • Level 7: Entry-Level Postgraduate Qualifications
    • What it means: Postgraduate certificate/diploma or a Master's by coursework in some fields.
    • Examples: Postgraduate Certificate in a specific discipline, Postgraduate Diploma.

SLQF L-8-10: Advanced Postgraduate Qualifications

  • Level 8: Master's Degree (Coursework & Research)
    • What it means: Advanced study combining coursework with a substantial research component.
    • Example: Master of Science in Environmental Science with a research project (minimum 60 credits, including at least 15 for research).
  • Level 9: Master of Philosophy (MPhil)
    • What it means: Research-intensive postgraduate qualification serving as a bridge to doctoral studies.
    • Example: MPhil in Environmental Science.
  • Level 10: Doctoral Degree (PhD)
    • What it means: The highest academic qualification with original research contribution.
    • Example: PhD in any discipline.
